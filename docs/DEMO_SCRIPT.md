# ProofMesh Demo Recording Script

## Recording target

- Length: 2 minutes 45 seconds to 3 minutes 15 seconds
- Viewport: desktop browser at 1440×900 or larger
- Language: English narration is recommended for Demo Day
- Input: use the preloaded Great Wall claim for the stable rehearsal; use a live claim only after the Gonka key has been verified
- Before recording: close unrelated tabs, hide bookmarks, disable notifications, and confirm the ProofMesh page starts at the top

## One-take shot list and narration

### 0:00–0:18 — The problem

**On screen:** ProofMesh hero and investigation composer.

**Say:**

> Every day, a viral claim reaches us before the evidence does. Existing fact-checkers give us a verdict, but they rarely show how the verdict was built. ProofMesh turns any disputed claim into an auditable case file.

### 0:18–0:38 — The product promise

**On screen:** Point to the four input modes, then the “2 models / Live evidence / Audit IDs” row.

**Say:**

> I can submit an article, an X post, a text claim, or an image. ProofMesh separates the content from instructions, maps the atomic claims, gathers evidence, and sends the same evidence packet to two different models on GonkaRouter.

### 0:38–0:58 — Start the investigation

**On screen:** Use the preloaded claim, then click **Investigate claim**.

**Say:**

> Let’s investigate a familiar claim: that the Great Wall of China is visible from the Moon with the naked eye. Kimi acts as the Investigator, while MiniMax acts as the Challenger.

**Pause narration for two seconds** while the four pipeline stages animate.

### 0:58–1:30 — Reveal the verdict

**On screen:** The report scrolls to the central claim and Truth Score.

**Say:**

> ProofMesh does not hide behind a binary answer. It gives this claim a Truth Score of six, marks it false with calibrated confidence, and explains the crucial distinction between lunar visibility and photography from low Earth orbit.

### 1:30–1:55 — Atomic claims and model disagreement

**On screen:** Scroll through **Claim map** and **Evidence duel**.

**Say:**

> The original sentence becomes three independently testable claims. Both models receive the same evidence, but they have opposing mandates. The Investigator builds the strongest supported interpretation; the Challenger looks for ambiguity, missing context, and unsupported leaps.

### 1:55–2:20 — Auditability

**On screen:** Pause on the three model cards and their request IDs.

**Say:**

> Every inference is routed through GonkaRouter, and every participating model and request ID remains visible. The conclusion is not a black box: a reviewer can see who reasoned, what evidence was available, and where the models agreed or disagreed.

### 2:20–2:42 — Evidence and timeline

**On screen:** Scroll through **Evidence trail** and **Truth timeline**.

**Say:**

> ProofMesh keeps source provenance visible and scores source diversity. The Truth Timeline shows how the verdict changes as stronger evidence enters the case, turning truth from a static label into a transparent process.

### 2:42–2:58 — Falsifiability

**On screen:** Pause on **What would change this verdict?**

**Say:**

> Most importantly, every report states what new evidence would change the verdict. ProofMesh is reversible by design, because trustworthy systems should explain how they could be proven wrong.

### 2:58–3:10 — Shareable output

**On screen:** Scroll back to the report header and click **Download Proof Card**.

**Say:**

> The final case can be exported as a Proof Card for social media, community moderation, or newsroom workflows. ProofMesh is not another AI answer. It is an auditable truth layer for the open web.

## Recording acceptance checklist

- The opening frame shows the complete headline and investigation composer.
- The selected input is visible before clicking **Investigate claim**.
- All four analysis stages animate.
- The result shows a Truth Score, verdict, and confidence.
- Kimi and MiniMax cards are visible with Gonka request IDs.
- At least two evidence sources are legible.
- “What would change this verdict?” appears in the recording.
- Proof Card export succeeds.
- The footer or final narration mentions GonkaRouter.
- No API key, email address, browser account menu, or private notification appears on screen.

## Backup plan

If the live API is unavailable during recording, reload the page without the secret and use the clearly labeled curated demo simulation. Do not describe demo request IDs as live network calls. The final competition recording should preferably use a successful live Gonka run.
